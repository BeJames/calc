Fourier Transform Calculator

This calculator performs a Fourier Transform on a waveform. The Fourier Transform, named after Jean-Baptiste Joseph Fourier, decomposes a waveform into the frequencies that make it up. In simple terms, it takes a complex signal and breaks it down into its basic building blocks.

The Fourier Transform was initially used in heat transfer physics but has found various applications in the digital world. Its early commercial applications include image processing, audio signal processing, and radar technologies.

The Output Fundamental Frequency

The output represents the fundamental frequency of the input wave, which is the frequency with the most energy. It is calculated by finding the index with the highest amplitude in the Fourier Transform and converting this index to a frequency.

The Phase Spectrum

Phase Spectrum: Each component frequency also has a phase associated with it. The phase represents the "offset" or the starting point of each component sine wave. We can plot this phase information as a separate chart.

The Power Spectrum

Power Spectrum: Is obtained by squaring the amplitude of each of the frequency components obtained by the Fourier Transform. It gives the power present at each frequency.
